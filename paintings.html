Some of the Most Expensive Paintings in the World

Salvator Mundi

Salvator Mundi was sold for $450.3 million in 2017, which is the highest price any painting has ever sold for. It was painted around the early 1500s by Leonardo da Vinci. The painting shows Christ holding a crystal orb.

Interchange

Interchange is the second most expensive painting ever sold, at about $300 million. It was painted by Willem de Kooning in 1955. It’s an abstract painting, but it’s supposed to represent a seated woman.

The Card Players

The third most expensive painting is The Card Players. It was sold for over $250 million in 2011. It was painted during 1892 and 1893 by Paul Cézanne. As the name suggests, the painting features two men playing cards.

Nafea Faa Ipoipo

Nafea Faa Ipoipo was sold for $210 million in 2014, making it the 4th most expensive painting. It was painted by Paul Gauguin in 1892, and shows two Tahitian women in a landscape. The name is in Tahitian, and it translates to “When Will You Marry?”.

Number 17A

Number 17A is one of Jackson Pollock’s most famous paintings. It was painted in 1948 and sold for about $200 million in 2015. It is an abstract painting created by using a drip technique.

No. 6 (Violet, Green, and Red)

No. 6 (Violet, Green, and Red) is an abstract painting by Mark Rothko that sold for €140 million ($186 million) in 2014. It was painted in 1951, and although this painting looks very simple, it has had an influence on contemporary art.

Masterpiece

Masterpiece was painted by Roy Lichtenstein in 1962, and sold for $165 million in 2017. It’s a form of pop art, and was inspired by comic books. It shows a woman telling an artist how amazing his painting is, and it even has dialogue!

Portrait of Dr. Gachet

Portrait of Dr. Gachet was sold for $82.5 million all the way back in 1990, which makes it worth almost double the price in today’s money! It was painted by Vincent van Gogh, one of the world’s most famous artists, in 1890. It shows Paul Gachet leaning his head on his right arm. Gachet was the person who took care of van Gogh during the last few months of his life.

Rabbit

Rabbit isn’t a painting, it’s a 3-foot stainless steel sculpture, but it sold for $91.1 million in 2019. It was made by Jeff Koons in 1986. This sculpture is significant because it’s the most expensive artwork sold by a living artist at an auction.
